Colour theory rationale

Why these colours, and why the designer's original aqua secondary doesn't work.

Primary: Magenta #DE0387

HSL ≈ 322°, 98%, 44%. A high-chroma, warm-leaning magenta — bold, energetic, and unmistakably the hero colour. At full saturation it's a strong CTA anchor. This sits in the red-violet range on the colour wheel.

Why the aqua #00D4FF fails as a secondary

The original aqua sits at roughly 191°, 100%, 50% — a fully saturated cyan. Two problems: first, both colours are at maximum chroma (98% and 100% saturation respectively), so they compete for dominance instead of establishing hierarchy. Second, they're near-split-complementary (131° apart), which creates a jarring thermal clash — hot magenta versus icy cyan — that reads as visual noise rather than intentional contrast.

In UI, a secondary colour must recede. A secondary button at full aqua saturation demands the same visual weight as the primary CTA, destroying the hierarchy your users need to navigate quickly — especially on a mobile-first cycling app where decisions happen in seconds.

Secondary: Muted Deep Teal #266A7C

Instead of fighting the magenta, the secondary is a desaturated deep teal at HSL ≈ 194°, 48%, 32%. This keeps the cool-warm polarity that makes the original pairing interesting, but tames the secondary to roughly half the primary's saturation and drops its lightness significantly. The result: the teal feels like a supporting voice — present enough to create visual variety, quiet enough to never upstage the magenta.

The 194° hue is also a near-complementary split to 322°, which colour theory favours for balanced, harmonious palettes. The low saturation prevents it from reading as "competing brand colour" and instead reads as "trusted, stable, institutional" — exactly what secondary actions should feel like.

Neutrals: Warm Slate

Pure #000000 and #FFFFFF are harsh — they create maximum contrast that's fatiguing over long sessions. The neutrals here carry a faint warm undertone (shifted toward the magenta's red-violet neighbourhood) so backgrounds and text feel cohesive with the brand palette rather than clinically stark.

Off-white #F8F6F7 has a barely perceptible pink warmth. Off-black #1A1517 is a deep charcoal with a subtle reddish base. Together they create a softer, more premium foundation than pure black/white while maintaining excellent readability.

State colours: Separated from the brand

Error uses a true red (#DC2626) deliberately pushed away from the magenta primary — if error and primary overlap in hue, users can't distinguish "action" from "danger" at a glance. Success uses a warm green shifted slightly toward yellow to avoid clashing with the cool teal. Warning is warm amber. Info is a muted slate-blue, cool and neutral, separated from both primary and secondary hues.
